About

I build things that make complex data accessible. Currently deep-diving into innovative visualization techniques through my chess project—transforming traditional board positions into D3 graphs that reveal tactical patterns like discovered attacks and checkmate sequences.

This work challenges the limitations of conventional data visualization, exploring how we can better represent multidimensional relationships. The lessons learned here apply beyond chess—from traffic flow analysis to system architecture mapping.

Current Focus

Chess Data Visualization Lab

An experimental platform pushing the boundaries of chess visualization. Building iterative display methods that transform positions into D3 graphs, revealing tactical concepts through novel representations. Each visualization serves as a testbed for broader data visualization challenges—some succeed brilliantly at isolating specific patterns, while others deliberately expose the limits of what visualization can meaningfully convey.
